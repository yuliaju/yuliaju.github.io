Thoughts - 1/13

Posted on January 13, 2016
Thoughts:
  • The importance of common knowledge1 in interactions. Common knowledge is the lower bound for effective communication. Sexual tension is an example of not common knowledge.
  • Aligning reactions to better train decision-making. Example of the poker player who mentally rewards themselves for good decision-making, not winning/losing.

    • Never regret positive expected value

    — Julia (@helloojuliaa) December 24, 2015
  • Integrated/centralized everything (to-do lists, data collection, etc.) would be nice, especially for people who think more data = better. This is the mission of WeChat, IFTTT, Vurb.
  • Cohortly. Michael and I tried to come up with a concise and specific way of qualifying non-general comparisons. For example, if I say "My math skills are below average," I'm comparing myself to a very specific group, not the whole population. Adding "cohortly" (or a better alternative) to the comparison gives me a way to communicate that.
  • Thinking about how to learn: rigorizing intuition-building, trying to retroactively spot mental blocks. Realized that learning is all about moving System 2 knowledge to System 1 intuition. Noticed that only 2/10 of my STEM teachers at Princeton so far have tried to teach intuition, and the others seem to barely think about effective teaching. Sad.

Updates:
  • Having a regular sleep schedule would be much more convenient for me, since I like to schedule out my days. Started a 1 AM-10 AM sleep schedule this week; will probably adjust this down to 8 h.
  • I should learn history to feed my System 1 more data2.
  • Small update toward "I can write programs to solve some of my problems."
  • System 1 update on how cool and powerful VR is after reading this.
