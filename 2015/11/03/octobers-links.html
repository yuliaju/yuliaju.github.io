What I've been reading this month:

  • Research on incompetence: "The incompetent don't perform up to speed; don't recognise their lack of competence; and don't even recognise the competence of other people."
  • Quantifying fluid intelligence using MRI.
  • Case study on architecture and social development in mid-20th century St. Louis.
  • Insights into pick-up artistry/texting
  • Advances in camera tech, the "first multi-aperture computational camera."
  • So fascinating to read about drastically different lifestyles—in this case, those of super rich Chinese children.
  • A social credit score for political alignment in China; apparently this will become mandatory in 2020.
  • Interview with the founder of Coursera on learning, personal missions, automation, and other interesting topics.
  • So awesome—"a low-cost smartphone accessory that…simultaneously detects three infectious disease markers—HIV and syphilis—from a finger prick of blood in just 15 minutes."
